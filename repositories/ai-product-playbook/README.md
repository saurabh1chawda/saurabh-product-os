# AI Product Playbook

Status: v1 repository foundation  
Last updated: July 2026  
Product Family: AI Product Management  
Product Collection: AI Product Collection

AI Product Playbook is a practical operating guide for identifying, validating, designing, measuring, and scaling AI products based on customer value, trust, and measurable outcomes.

It helps Product Managers avoid starting with model novelty and instead make disciplined product decisions around workflows, evidence, automation depth, trust, readiness, and business value.

## Executive Summary

AI products do not succeed because they use advanced models. They succeed when they solve meaningful customer problems with enough reliability, trust, and business value to change real workflows.

This repository is designed as a reusable product resource for Product Managers, AI Product Managers, product leaders, and cross-functional teams who need to evaluate whether AI belongs in a product, how much automation is appropriate, and what evidence should guide investment.

## Problem Statement

Many AI product efforts begin with a technology assumption:

- We should add AI because competitors did.
- We should use an LLM because it is available.
- We should automate because the demo looks impressive.

That approach often creates products with weak adoption, unclear business impact, poor trust, expensive operations, and fragile user experiences. Teams need a practical playbook that connects AI decisions to customer workflows, risk, measurement, and product strategy.

## What You'll Learn

- How to decide whether an opportunity is actually an AI problem.
- How to prioritize AI opportunities by customer value, business value, and readiness.
- How to choose between traditional software, RAG, agents, and hybrid systems.
- How to evaluate build, buy, configure, extend, or platform decisions.
- How to design trust safeguards before increasing automation.
- How to connect AI system metrics to customer and business outcomes.
- How to use examples from platform, workflow, payments, and growth products without copying case-study narratives.
