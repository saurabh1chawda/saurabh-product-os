# Logix AI Product Lifecycle Example

Status: Complete end-to-end worked example  
Decision: Modernize the platform before teaching it to think

## Executive Summary

The Logix journey shows how the Product Leadership Platform works as an ecosystem.

The product decision was not to ship visible AI features first. It was to modernize the platform foundation, improve mobile-first customer experience, strengthen delivery velocity, and create the conditions for reliable AI execution.

This example demonstrates how discovery, prioritization, architecture, decision writing, leadership evidence, business outcomes, and lessons connect across repositories.

## Discovery

Problem signal: demand for AI-powered capabilities was increasing, but the platform had technical debt, fragmented customer journeys, slow release cycles, and desktop-first friction.

Discovery question: was the highest-leverage opportunity to ship AI immediately, or to improve the platform that future AI would depend on?

Repository handoff:

- Product Discovery Toolkit identifies the customer and workflow constraints.
- Evidence should include customer friction, release bottlenecks, conversion signals, and platform reliability constraints.

Output:

- Customer workflow constraint.
- Platform readiness gap.
- Initial assumption: AI value depends on platform readiness.

## Prioritization

Opportunity: build an AI-ready product foundation before expanding advanced AI features.

Prioritization logic:

- Customer value: better experience and reliability.
- Business value: stronger conversion, engagement, and recurring revenue.
- Readiness: platform modernization improves execution readiness.
- Risk: visible AI without platform maturity could amplify existing weaknesses.

Repository handoff:

- AI Prioritization Framework evaluates whether the opportunity deserves investment.

Output:

- Recommendation to prioritize platform modernization and AI readiness before advanced AI expansion.

## Architecture

Architecture question: should the team patch the current system, rewrite from scratch, buy a platform, or modernize incrementally?

Decision path:

- Reject full rewrite because migration and revenue risk were too high.
- Reject patch-only optimization because it preserved long-term constraints.
- Choose incremental modernization with mobile-first redesign and AI readiness.

Repository handoff:

- AI Product Playbook coordinates build-vs-buy, architecture, trust, measurement, and operating readiness decisions.

Output:

- Incremental modernization path.
- AI-ready foundation.
- Customer-facing experience improvements.

## Decision Memo

Decision request: approve platform modernization before advanced AI feature expansion.

Recommendation: modernize the platform first because AI features would only create durable value if the underlying product could support reliable execution, measurement, and customer adoption.

Repository handoff:

- Decision Memo Template communicates the executive recommendation, alternatives, trade-offs, risks, and success criteria.

Output:

- Executive decision memo.
- Alternatives and trade-offs.
- Success metrics and review plan.

## Leadership Brief

Leadership decision: we modernized the platform before teaching it to think.

Repository handoff:

- Product Leadership Briefs documents the product judgment, stakeholder alignment, outcomes, and reusable principle.

Output:

- Product Leadership Brief.
- Leadership card.
- Reusable principle: platform before intelligence.

## Lessons Learned

- AI product strategy often starts with platform readiness.
- The best AI sequencing decision may delay visible AI features in favor of long-term leverage.
- Architecture decisions should be communicated as product decisions.
- Product leadership evidence improves when the decision, trade-off, and outcome are documented separately.
