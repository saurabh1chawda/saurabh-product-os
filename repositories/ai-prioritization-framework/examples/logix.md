# Logix Worked Example

Status: Complete worked example  
Related context: AI-ready platform modernization  
Recommended use: Roadmap workshop, AI investment review, executive product review

## Summary

Logix had commercial momentum and increasing demand for AI-powered capabilities, but the existing platform was constrained by technical debt, fragmented customer journeys, slow release cycles, and desktop-first experiences.

The prioritization question was not "Which AI feature should we build first?" It was "Is the platform ready for AI investment, or should foundation work be prioritized first?"

## Customer Problem

Customers needed faster, simpler, more reliable digital workflows.

The product experience was constrained by:

- Desktop-first journeys that created friction.
- Slow release cycles that delayed improvements.
- Platform architecture that limited experimentation.
- Technical debt that reduced delivery speed.
- Emerging AI expectations that the platform could not yet support reliably.

## Opportunity

Create an AI-ready platform foundation that would make future AI capabilities scalable, reliable, measurable, and commercially useful.

This was not a recommendation to launch broad AI features immediately. It was a recommendation to prioritize the foundation required for durable AI leverage.

## Recommendation

Prototype.

More specifically: fund the AI-ready platform foundation and run constrained AI-readiness pilots. Do not prioritize broad customer-facing AI features until execution readiness and trust foundations improve.

## Expected Outcome

Expected outcomes from the recommendation:

- Faster release velocity.
- Improved customer experience.
- Stronger platform scalability.
- Better foundation for AI capabilities.
- Lower risk of superficial AI features.
- Improved ability to measure future AI value.

## Actual Outcome

Observed outcomes from the Product OS Logix brief:

- Rs1M+ ARR.
- 20% MRR growth.
- 25% engagement increase.
- 2x organic traffic.
- 3x delivery velocity.
- 2x faster releases.
- 15% system throughput increase.

## Lessons

1. AI prioritization is often platform prioritization.
2. A high-value AI opportunity can still require foundation work before visible AI features.
3. Execution readiness and trust risk should constrain AI ambition.
4. The right recommendation may be "Prototype" even when business value is strong.
5. Product leaders should prioritize the highest-leverage constraint, not the most visible AI feature.
