# AI Prioritization Framework

Status: v1 repository foundation  
Last updated: July 2026  
Product Family: AI Product Management  
Product Collection: AI Product Collection

AI Prioritization Framework helps Product Managers evaluate whether an AI opportunity deserves investment.

The framework emphasizes decision quality over AI enthusiasm. It gives teams a practical way to compare opportunities by customer value, business value, execution readiness, and trust risk before committing product, engineering, data, or go-to-market resources.

## Executive Summary

Not every product problem should become an AI product.

This repository provides a reusable prioritization framework for deciding whether an AI opportunity should be built now, prototyped, validated further, parked, or rejected. It is designed for roadmap planning, product strategy reviews, AI portfolio discussions, and executive prioritization conversations.

## Problem Statement

AI roadmaps often become crowded with ideas that sound impressive but lack durable customer value.

Common failure patterns include:

- Building because competitors launched AI.
- Prioritizing demos over workflows.
- Ignoring data readiness.
- Underestimating operational cost.
- Treating model feasibility as the only decision factor.
- Scaling before customer behavior proves value.

Teams need a practical way to evaluate AI opportunities before investment increases.

## Why This Framework Exists

The framework exists to help teams make AI product decisions with evidence, not hype.

It turns broad questions like "Should we build this AI feature?" into a structured review of customer pain, business value, strategic fit, data readiness, model feasibility, operational complexity, trust risk, and time to value.

## Framework Overview

The AI Prioritization Framework follows this flow:

1. Define the AI opportunity.
2. Clarify the customer workflow and pain.
3. Evaluate business and strategic value.
4. Assess data, model, operational, and trust readiness.
5. Score the opportunity.
6. Apply anti-pattern checks.
7. Decide whether to build, prototype, validate further, park, or reject.

See [Framework](framework/framework.md).

## Four Evaluation Dimensions

The framework groups evaluation criteria into four decision dimensions:

| Dimension | What It Tests |
| --- | --- |
| Customer Value | Is the problem painful, frequent, and meaningful enough to change behavior? |
| Business Value | Does the opportunity support revenue, retention, cost, strategic leverage, or differentiation? |
| Execution Readiness | Can the team build, evaluate, operate, and support the capability responsibly? |
| Trust & Risk | Would customers trust the system, and can the team manage error, control, compliance, and fallback paths? |

See [Evaluation Criteria](framework/evaluation-criteria.md).

## Scoring Model

The scoring model uses weighted judgment rather than mechanical math. Scores are useful only when they create better discussion.

Recommended actions:

- Build Now
- Prototype
- Validate Further
- Park
- Reject

See [Scoring Model](framework/scoring-model.md) and [Recommendation Guide](framework/recommendation-guide.md).
