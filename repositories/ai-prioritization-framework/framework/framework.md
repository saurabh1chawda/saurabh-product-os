# AI Prioritization Framework

Status: v1 framework  
Last updated: July 2026

## Purpose

The AI Prioritization Framework helps teams decide whether an AI opportunity deserves investment.

The goal is not to prove that AI is exciting. The goal is to determine whether AI is the right product investment for a specific customer workflow, business outcome, and operating context.

## End-to-End Process

### 1. Define the Opportunity

Describe the AI opportunity in plain product language.

Avoid starting with model capability. Start with the customer problem, workflow, and desired behavior change.

### 2. Clarify the Customer Workflow

Identify:

- Who the user is
- What workflow they are completing
- Where friction appears
- How often the problem occurs
- What failure costs the customer
- What behavior would change if the solution worked

### 3. Evaluate Business Value

Clarify the business reason to invest.

Examples:

- Revenue growth
- Retention
- Expansion
- Cost reduction
- Differentiation
- Platform leverage
- Workflow adoption

### 4. Assess Execution Readiness

Review whether the team can build and operate the capability.

Consider:

- Data readiness
- Model feasibility
- Evaluation loop
- Engineering complexity
- Operational support
- Cost at scale
- Measurement clarity

### 5. Evaluate Trust and Risk

Ask whether customers would trust the system and whether the team can manage failure.

Consider:

- Human control
- Explainability
- Error tolerance
- Auditability
- Fallback paths
- Regulatory sensitivity
- Reversibility

### 6. Score the Opportunity

Use the scoring sheet to compare opportunities. Scores should guide conversation, not replace judgment.

### 7. Apply Anti-Pattern Checks

Before recommending investment, review situations where AI should not be prioritized.

See [Anti-Patterns](anti-patterns.md).

### 8. Make the Recommendation

Choose one:

- Build Now
- Prototype
- Validate Further
- Park
- Reject

## Output

The output should be a clear product recommendation with:

- Opportunity summary
- Score
- Primary rationale
- Key risks
- Recommended next step
- Decision owner
- Review date
