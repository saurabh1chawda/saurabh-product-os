# Evaluation Criteria

Status: v1 criteria  
Last updated: July 2026

The framework evaluates opportunities across four dimensions.

## 1. Customer Value

Question: Does this opportunity solve a meaningful customer problem?

Criteria:

- Customer Pain
- Workflow Frequency
- Cost of Failure
- Behavior Change Potential
- Existing Workaround Strength

Strong signal:

- Users face frequent friction.
- The workflow is important.
- The problem has measurable cost.
- Customers would change behavior if the product worked.

Weak signal:

- The problem is rare.
- The pain is mild.
- AI would create novelty but not behavior change.

## 2. Business Value

Question: Does this opportunity create measurable business value?

Criteria:

- Revenue Impact
- Retention Impact
- Expansion Potential
- Cost Reduction
- Strategic Alignment
- Differentiation

Strong signal:

- The opportunity connects to a strategic business outcome.
- Success can be measured.
- Value compounds across customers, workflows, or products.

Weak signal:

- The opportunity is disconnected from business priorities.
- Success is described as usage only.
- The business case depends on vague AI positioning.

## 3. Execution Readiness

Question: Can the team build, evaluate, launch, and operate this responsibly?

Criteria:

- Data Readiness
- Model Feasibility
- Evaluation Loop
- Engineering Complexity
- Operational Support
- Cost at Scale
- Measurement Clarity

Strong signal:

- Required data exists and is usable.
- Quality can be evaluated.
- The workflow can be supported operationally.
- Costs are proportional to value.

Weak signal:

- Data quality is poor.
- Evaluation is unclear.
- Costs are unknown.
- Operations cannot support failure cases.

## 4. Trust & Risk

Question: Would customers trust this capability, and can the team manage failure?

Criteria:

- Error Tolerance
- Human Override
- Explainability
- Auditability
- Fallback Paths
- Regulatory Sensitivity
- Reversibility

Strong signal:

- Users remain in control where needed.
- Failures can be recovered.
- The system communicates uncertainty.
- The risk profile matches the automation level.

Weak signal:

- Incorrect output creates high harm.
- Users cannot override or inspect decisions.
- Failure paths are undefined.
- Compliance expectations are unclear.
