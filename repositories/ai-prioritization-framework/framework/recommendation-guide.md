# Recommendation Guide

Status: v1 hardening guide  
Last updated: July 2026

## Purpose

The recommendation guide explains when to choose Build Now, Prototype, Validate Further, Park, or Reject.

Scores should guide the conversation, but recommendations should also reflect gating risks such as weak customer value, low readiness, or unmanaged trust risk.

## Score Bands

| Score | Default Recommendation | Meaning |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 85-100 | Build Now | Strong customer value, strong business value, high readiness, and manageable trust risk |
| 70-84 | Prototype | Strong opportunity, but one or more readiness, trust, data, or operating assumptions should be tested |
| 55-69 | Validate Further | Problem may matter, but evidence is not strong enough for roadmap commitment |
| 40-54 | Park | Opportunity is interesting, but timing, readiness, economics, or strategic fit are weak |
| 0-39 | Reject | AI creates more complexity than customer or business value |

## Gating Rules

Use these rules even when the total score looks strong:

- If Customer Value is below 3.0 / 5, do not choose Build Now.
- If Trust & Risk is below 3.0 / 5, do not choose Build Now.
- If Execution Readiness is below 2.5 / 5, choose Prototype or Validate Further.
- If the opportunity can be solved better with rules, UX, search, or workflow design, choose Reject or Park for AI.
- If success cannot be measured, choose Validate Further.

## Build Now

Choose Build Now when:

- Customer pain is meaningful and repeated.
- Business impact is measurable.
- Data, model, engineering, and operational readiness are strong.
- Trust risks are understood and manageable.
- The team knows what success, failure, and review timing look like.

Do not choose Build Now simply because the idea is strategically important.

## Prototype

Choose Prototype when:

- The upside is strong.
- One or more assumptions need practical testing.
- The team needs evidence on workflow fit, trust, data quality, cost, or user behavior.
- The cost of learning is proportional to the decision.

Prototype is the right choice when the team should learn before scaling.

## Validate Further

Choose Validate Further when:

- The problem may matter but evidence is thin.
- Customer behavior is not yet clear.
- Business value is plausible but unproven.
- Success criteria are not defined.
- The riskiest assumption has not been tested.

Validate Further is the right choice when the team is still clarifying the opportunity.

## Park

Choose Park when:

- The opportunity is interesting but not urgent.
- Timing is poor.
- Strategic fit is weak.
- Readiness gaps are large.
- Economics are unattractive today.

Parked opportunities should have explicit revisit conditions.

## Reject

Choose Reject when:

- There is no meaningful customer value.
- Simpler software would solve the problem better.
- Trust risk is unacceptable.
- Cost is high and impact is low.
- The team is pursuing AI novelty rather than workflow improvement.

Rejecting weak AI ideas protects product focus.
