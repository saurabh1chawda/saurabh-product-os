# Product Leadership Briefs

Status: v1 repository foundation  
Last updated: July 2026  
Product Family: Product Leadership  
Product Collection: Product Leadership Collection

Product Leadership Briefs is a reusable library for documenting difficult product decisions as structured, evidence-backed executive reviews.

It is not a portfolio and not a blog. It is a decision library designed to help product leaders, hiring managers, and Product Managers understand how product judgment becomes strategy, trade-offs, execution, measurable outcomes, and reusable leadership principles.

## Executive Summary

Strong product leadership is easiest to evaluate when decisions are visible.

This repository provides a repeatable Product Leadership Brief format for documenting the context, problem, options, decision, trade-offs, stakeholder alignment, results, lessons learned, and reusable principle behind significant product initiatives.

The goal is to make product judgment inspectable without requiring a long narrative case study.

## Problem Statement

Most case studies are hard to compare. They often emphasize storytelling, screenshots, or outcomes without showing the decision structure that produced those outcomes.

Hiring managers and product leaders need to see whether a Product Manager can:

- Frame ambiguous problems.
- Evaluate strategic options.
- Make trade-offs explicit.
- Align stakeholders.
- Sequence execution.
- Measure outcomes.
- Extract reusable principles from experience.

## Why This Repository Exists

Product OS renders polished Product Leadership Briefs as part of Saurabh Chawda's executive product leadership system.

This repository exposes the reusable method behind those briefs. It is meant to help other Product Managers document product decisions with more clarity, evidence, and executive usefulness.

**Secondary**

## Repository Philosophy

- Decision before narrative.
- Evidence before assertion.
- Trade-offs before celebration.
- Outcomes before output.
- Reflection before self-promotion.
- Reusable principles before one-off stories.

Every brief should answer one question:

Would I trust this person to lead a difficult product decision?

## Product Decision Framework

Every Product Leadership Brief follows a consistent structure:

1. Context
2. Problem
3. Options Considered
4. Decision
5. Trade-offs
6. Stakeholder Alignment
7. Results
8. Lessons Learned
9. Reusable Principle

See [Product Decision Framework](frameworks/product-decision-framework.md).
