# JoVE Product Leadership Brief

Status: Available in Product OS  
Category: Customer Experience, AI, Product Strategy  
Decision: Workflow adoption over content expansion

## Executive Summary

We prioritized workflow adoption over content expansion.

Researchers and educators were not abandoning the platform because the product lacked scientific content. The higher-leverage problem was that the product did not sufficiently support how users found, applied, and embedded content inside real teaching, research, and laboratory workflows.

The decision shifted investment from more content toward workflow adoption, improving portfolio revenue, session duration, page views, and video consumption.

## Business Context

JoVE served research, education, and biopharma audiences using scientific video content to support learning, research, and workflow execution.

The product had valuable content, but adoption depended on whether users could integrate that content into their work.

## Decision Timeline

| Stage | Decision Signal | Product Response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Content growth | More scientific content looked like the obvious path | Test whether content volume was the real adoption constraint |
| Discovery | Users needed workflow fit, not only more assets | Reframe adoption around workflow completion |
| Strategy | Workflow support created stronger product leverage | Prioritize discovery, context, and application paths |
| Outcome | Engagement and revenue signals improved | Treat workflow adoption as the reusable principle |

## Stakeholder Map

| Stakeholder | Optimized For | Concern | Alignment Move |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Product | Adoption and portfolio growth | Content alone may not change behavior | Reframe around workflow adoption |
| Content teams | Scientific quality and coverage | Less emphasis on new content volume | Connect content value to workflow use |
| Customers | Easier teaching, research, and application | Product must fit existing workflows | Improve discovery and task completion |
| Leadership | Revenue and engagement growth | Need measurable business impact | Link workflow adoption to portfolio metrics |

## Options Considered

| Option | Benefit | Risk | Decision |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Produce more content | Clear supply-side expansion | May not solve adoption friction | Rejected as primary path |
| Improve content discovery only | Better findability | Still may stop before workflow completion | Partial input |
| Prioritize workflow adoption | Stronger customer behavior change | Requires cross-functional product strategy | Chosen |

## Decision Matrix

| Dimension | Content Expansion | Workflow Adoption |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Customer impact | Medium | High |

| Strategic importance | Medium | High |
| Learning value | Medium | High |

## Risks

- Content stakeholders may perceive reduced investment in core assets.
- Workflow changes may require deeper discovery and iteration.
- Engagement improvements must connect to business outcomes.

## Outcomes

The decision improved product adoption signals by focusing on how customers used content inside their workflow rather than treating content volume as the main lever.

## Metrics

| Metric | Outcome |
| --- | --- |
| Portfolio revenue | +30% |
| Session duration | +40% |
| Page views | +15% |
| Video consumption | +12% |

## Lessons Learned

- More supply does not automatically create more adoption.
- Workflow fit can be more valuable than feature or content expansion.
- Discovery should identify the constraint behind behavior, not just the visible request.

## Reusable Principle

Adoption before expansion.

Growth compounds when customers successfully adopt the product already built.
