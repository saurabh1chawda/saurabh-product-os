# Brief Scorecard

Status: v1 framework  
Last updated: July 2026

## Purpose

The Brief Scorecard evaluates whether a Product Leadership Brief teaches a decision well enough to be useful beyond the original company context.

Use it when reviewing a brief for coaching, interview preparation, executive review, or product strategy workshops.

## Scorecard Dimensions

Score each dimension from 1 to 5.

| Dimension | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Customer Impact | Did the decision improve a meaningful customer workflow or experience? |
| Business Impact | Did the decision connect to measurable business outcomes? |
| Technical Complexity | Did the brief explain platform, architecture, data, reliability, or execution complexity clearly? |
| Strategic Importance | Did the decision affect sequencing, investment, market position, or long-term leverage? |
| Evidence Strength | Did the brief show why the decision was justified? |
| Learning Value | Can another Product Manager reuse the lesson in a different context? |

## Scoring Guidance

| Score | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Weak signal or unclear relevance |
| 2 | Some relevance, limited evidence |
| 3 | Useful but context-dependent |
| 4 | Strong evidence and transferable learning |
| 5 | Flagship-quality decision with clear teaching value |

## Interpretation

| Total | Interpretation |
| --- | --- |
| 25-30 | Flagship brief |
| 19-24 | Strong teaching brief |
| 13-18 | Useful but needs more evidence |
| 6-12 | Reframe before publishing |
