# Discovery Process

Status: v1 framework  
Last updated: July 2026

## Purpose

The Discovery Process helps teams move from problem signal to decision-ready evidence.

Discovery is not a phase for gathering opinions. It is a structured practice for reducing uncertainty before product investment increases.

## Process

### 1. Capture the Signal

Start with the trigger:

- Customer request
- Support issue
- Sales feedback
- Usage drop
- Churn signal
- Workflow observation
- Strategic opportunity

### 2. Define the Customer Workflow

Clarify:

- Who is the customer?
- What are they trying to accomplish?
- What happens before and after the problem?
- Where does friction appear?
- How often does it occur?
- What is the cost of failure?

### 3. Separate Requests From Needs

A request describes what someone wants built.

A need explains why the current workflow fails.

Discovery should move from request to need before solution planning begins.

### 4. Map Assumptions

Identify assumptions about:

- Customer pain
- Workflow frequency
- Business impact
- Technical feasibility
- Adoption behavior
- Willingness to pay or switch
- Risk and trust

### 5. Gather Evidence

Use customer interviews, behavioral data, support patterns, sales notes, product analytics, prototypes, experiments, or workflow observation.

### 6. Synthesize Learning

Look for:

- Repeated patterns
- Contradictions
- Segment differences
- Strong workarounds
- Decision triggers
- Evidence gaps

### 7. Decide the Next Move

Choose one:

- Build
- Prototype
- Experiment
- Reframe
- Continue Discovery
- Stop

## Output

The output should be a clear discovery decision:

- Problem statement
- Evidence summary
- Key assumptions
- Confidence level
- Recommended next step
- Decision owner
- Review date
