# Opportunity Assessment

Status: v1 framework  
Last updated: July 2026

## Purpose

Opportunity Assessment helps teams decide whether a customer problem deserves product investment.

## Assessment Dimensions

### Customer Pain

Is the problem painful enough for customers to change behavior?

Strong signals:

- Customers mention the problem without prompting.
- The problem appears repeatedly across interviews or behavior.
- Customers use workarounds.
- Failure has visible cost.

### Workflow Frequency

Does the problem occur often enough to matter?

Strong signals:

- The workflow is repeated.
- The problem appears at a critical moment.
- The pain compounds over time.

### Business Impact

Does solving the problem support business outcomes?

Strong signals:

- Retention, conversion, expansion, cost, efficiency, or adoption could improve.
- The outcome is measurable.
- The opportunity aligns with strategy.

### Solution Uncertainty

Do we understand what solution would work?

Strong signals:

- The customer problem is clear.
- Assumptions are mapped.
- The riskiest assumption can be tested.

### Readiness

Is the team ready to act?

Strong signals:

- Evidence is strong enough to prioritize.
- Dependencies are known.
- Success criteria are defined.

## Recommendation

Use one of:

- Ready for prioritization
- Needs validation
- Needs reframing
- Not worth pursuing now
