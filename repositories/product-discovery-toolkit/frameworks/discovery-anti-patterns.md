# Discovery Anti-Patterns

Status: v1 framework  
Last updated: July 2026

Discovery anti-patterns are common behaviors that make teams feel informed while leaving the real product decision under-evidenced.

## 1. Starting With the Solution

Symptom: The team asks customers whether they want a specific feature.

Risk: Customers react to the idea instead of revealing the workflow problem.

Better move: Ask about the current workflow, pain, workaround, and decision trigger.

## 2. Treating Requests as Needs

Symptom: A customer request becomes a roadmap item.

Risk: The team builds the requested solution without understanding the underlying need.

Better move: Ask what problem the request would solve and how the customer handles it today.

## 3. Overweighting the Loudest Customer

Symptom: One urgent customer or executive escalation drives roadmap priority.

Risk: The team optimizes for urgency rather than repeated value.

Better move: Compare the request against patterns across segments and business impact.

## 4. Confirmation Interviews

Symptom: Interviews are used to confirm a planned roadmap decision.

Risk: The team collects supportive quotes instead of testing assumptions.

Better move: Define the riskiest assumption before the interview.

## 5. Ignoring Behavior

Symptom: The team relies only on what customers say.

Risk: Stated intent does not translate into adoption.

Better move: Look for observed workflows, analytics, workarounds, and prototype interactions.

## 6. Skipping Synthesis

Symptom: Notes exist, but no decision-quality insight emerges.

Risk: Discovery becomes documentation rather than product learning.

Better move: Synthesize patterns, contradictions, segments, assumptions, and recommended decisions.

## 7. Defining Success After Launch

Symptom: Success criteria are decided after the solution ships.

Risk: The team cannot tell whether discovery improved the decision.

Better move: Define success, failure, and mixed-signal responses before build.
