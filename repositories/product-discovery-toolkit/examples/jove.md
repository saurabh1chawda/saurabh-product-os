# JoVE Worked Example

Status: Complete worked example  
Related context: Workflow adoption before content expansion  
Recommended use: Discovery sprint, opportunity assessment workshop, roadmap planning

## Summary

JoVE had strong scientific content, but customer adoption depended on whether researchers and instructors could integrate that content into real academic and laboratory workflows.

The discovery question was not "Should we build more content?" It was "What prevents users from adopting the product inside the workflow where learning, teaching, and research actually happen?"

## Business Context

The product operated in a scientific education and research environment where engagement, institutional adoption, renewal confidence, and portfolio revenue mattered.

The business needed stronger product adoption, not just more content inventory.

## Initial Assumptions

| Assumption | Type | Starting Belief |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Users needed more scientific videos | Customer | More content would increase engagement |
| Content discovery was the main adoption barrier | Product | Better findability would improve usage |
| Richer content would improve institutional value | Business | More assets would support renewal confidence |
| Workflow integration was secondary | Product | Users would adapt if content quality was strong |

## Discovery Objectives

1. Understand how researchers, instructors, and students used JoVE inside real workflows.
2. Identify where adoption failed after users understood the content.
3. Separate content gaps from workflow gaps.
4. Determine whether workflow support would create stronger engagement than content expansion.
5. Clarify which product decision should guide roadmap investment.

## Customer Interview Approach

Discovery should focus on workflow reconstruction, not feature preference.

Interview prompts:

- Walk me through the last time you used scientific video content in teaching, learning, or research.
- What happened before you searched for content?
- What happened after you found it?
- Where did the workflow break down?
- What did you need to do outside the platform?
- How did you decide whether the content was useful?
- What would make this easier to repeat?

Research targets:

- Researchers.
- Instructors.
- Students.
- Institutional buyers or champions.
- Customer-facing internal teams.

## Evidence Collected

| Evidence Type | Signal | Interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Interview signal | Users valued content but struggled to connect it to workflows | Content value did not automatically create adoption |
| Workflow signal | Users needed support around syllabus mapping, protocol use, and task completion | Product stopped before the user's job was done |
| Behavioral signal | Engagement improved when discovery and workflow fit improved | Workflow adoption was a better lever than content quantity alone |
| Business signal | Stronger engagement supported institutional adoption and renewal confidence | Workflow fit had business value |

## Assumptions Validated

- Users valued scientific content.
- Content discovery mattered.
- Engagement was connected to institutional value.
- Product experience could influence adoption beyond content quality.

## Assumptions Rejected

- More content alone would solve the adoption problem.
- Users would adapt their workflows around the product.
- Richer video features were the highest-leverage next investment.
- Understanding the content was enough to create repeated adoption.

## Opportunity Assessment

| Dimension | Assessment |
| --- | --- |
| Customer Pain | Users needed help applying content inside teaching and research workflows |
| Workflow Frequency | Workflows repeated across courses, research tasks, and institutional use cases |
| Business Impact | Better adoption could improve engagement, revenue, and renewal confidence |
| Evidence Quality | Repeated workflow signals were stronger than content requests alone |
| Readiness | The opportunity was ready for product strategy and workflow-focused roadmap decisions |

## Recommendation

Prioritize workflow adoption over content expansion.

This means investing in product experiences that help users map content to real tasks, discover relevant materials in context, and complete workflows more successfully.

## Outcome

Product OS records the JoVE outcome as:

- 30% portfolio revenue impact.
- 40% session duration improvement.
- 15% page view improvement.
- 12% video consumption improvement.

The important discovery lesson is that business impact followed workflow adoption, not content volume alone.

## Lessons Learned

1. Customer discovery should reconstruct workflows, not collect feature requests.
2. A content product can still have a workflow problem.
3. Adoption depends on where the product fits into the user's existing work.
4. Strong discovery changes the roadmap question.
5. Better evidence prevents teams from scaling the wrong investment.
