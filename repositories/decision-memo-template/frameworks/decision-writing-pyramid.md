# Decision Writing Pyramid

Status: v1 signature framework  
Last updated: July 2026

## Purpose

The Decision Writing Pyramid shows how executive decision documents should flow.

Most product memos start with background and eventually reach the recommendation. Executive readers need the opposite.

## Pyramid

```text
Recommendation
     |
Evidence
     |
Trade-offs
     |
Alternatives
     |
Context
```

## 1. Recommendation

Lead with the decision.

The reader should know what you want approved, changed, paused, funded, or rejected within the first minute.

## 2. Evidence

Show the strongest reasons for the recommendation.

Evidence should be specific, relevant, and decision-linked.

## 3. Trade-offs

Explain what the recommendation costs.

Trade-offs build trust because they show that the decision was not treated as obvious.

## 4. Alternatives

Summarize the other credible options.

This helps leaders see the decision space, not just the preferred path.

## 5. Context

Add only the background needed to understand the decision.

Context is important, but it should not bury the recommendation.

## Why This Improves Executive Readability

Executives read decision documents to make or validate a decision. The pyramid reduces cognitive load by giving them the recommendation first, then the evidence needed to inspect it.

This structure also improves review quality because disagreement can focus on the recommendation, evidence, trade-offs, or assumptions instead of getting lost in background.
