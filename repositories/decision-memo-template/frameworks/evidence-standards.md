# Evidence Standards

Status: v1 framework  
Last updated: July 2026

## Purpose

Evidence standards help memo readers distinguish between facts, assumptions, opinions, and risks.

## Evidence Types

| Type | Definition | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Customer evidence | Interview patterns, observed workflows, support themes, customer behavior | Shows whether the problem matters |
| Product evidence | Usage, funnel data, retention, adoption, workflow completion | Shows how users behave |
| Business evidence | Revenue, cost, margin, growth, retention, expansion | Shows why the decision matters commercially |
| Technical evidence | Architecture constraints, performance, reliability, data readiness | Shows feasibility and operating cost |
| Experiment evidence | Prototype results, A/B tests, pilots, fake doors, concierge tests | Shows whether assumptions hold |
| Stakeholder evidence | Sales, support, customer success, leadership context | Provides internal signal but needs validation |

## Evidence Quality

Strong evidence:

- Is linked to the decision.
- Is specific.
- Can be inspected.
- Shows behavior, not only opinion.
- Clarifies uncertainty.

Weak evidence:

- Is anecdotal without context.
- Confirms a preferred answer.
- Uses vague customer sentiment.
- Does not change the decision.

## Memo Rule

Every major recommendation should separate:

- What we know
- What we believe
- What remains uncertain
- What would change the decision
