# Common Memo Mistakes

Status: v1 framework  
Last updated: July 2026

These mistakes make product decisions harder to review, even when the underlying thinking is strong.

## 1. Recommendation Buried at the End

Problem: The reader must search for the decision.

Fix: Put the recommendation and decision request near the top.

## 2. Opinion Without Evidence

Problem: The memo argues from confidence instead of proof.

Fix: Separate recommendation, evidence, assumptions, and risks.

## 3. Missing Alternatives

Problem: The memo presents one path as if no other credible options existed.

Fix: Include the options considered and why they were rejected.

## 4. Hidden Assumptions

Problem: The decision depends on beliefs that are not visible.

Fix: Name the assumptions and identify which one is riskiest.

## 5. No Explicit Decision Request

Problem: Readers do not know whether they are being asked to approve, advise, fund, pause, or decide.

Fix: State the decision request at the top.

## 6. Excessive Background

Problem: Context overwhelms the decision.

Fix: Use the Decision Writing Pyramid: recommendation, evidence, trade-offs, alternatives, then context.

## 7. Trade-offs Hidden

Problem: The memo makes the recommendation look cost-free.

Fix: Explain what the team accepts, delays, protects, or gives up.

## 8. Success Criteria Missing

Problem: The team cannot evaluate whether the decision worked.

Fix: Define success, failure, and review timing before execution.
