# Decision Writing Framework

Status: v1 framework  
Last updated: July 2026

## Purpose

The Decision Writing Framework helps Product Managers communicate decisions clearly enough that stakeholders can evaluate the recommendation, evidence, trade-offs, and next action without needing another meeting.

## Structure

### 1. Decision Request

State the decision you need.

Examples:

- Approve this roadmap priority.
- Choose Option B over Option A.
- Fund a prototype.
- Pause investment until evidence improves.

### 2. Recommendation

State what you recommend and why.

The recommendation should be visible in the first few lines.

### 3. Evidence

Summarize the strongest evidence supporting the recommendation.

Use:

- Customer behavior
- Discovery findings
- Product analytics
- Business impact
- Technical constraints
- Experiment results

### 4. Alternatives

Show the credible options considered.

Avoid strawman alternatives. Strong memos demonstrate that other paths were taken seriously.

### 5. Trade-offs

Explain what the team accepts, delays, protects, or gives up.

### 6. Risks and Assumptions

Name what could make the recommendation wrong.

### 7. Success Criteria

Define what will prove the decision worked.

### 8. Follow-Up

Document owner, next step, review date, and decision log entry.

## Quality Bar

A strong decision memo lets a busy executive answer:

- What decision is being requested?
- Why is this the recommendation?
- What evidence supports it?
- What alternatives were considered?
- What trade-offs are we accepting?
- What happens next?
