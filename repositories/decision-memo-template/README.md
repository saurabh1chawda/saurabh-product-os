# Decision Memo Template

Status: v1 repository foundation  
Last updated: July 2026  
Product Family: Templates  
Product Collection: Product Toolkit Collection

Decision Memo Template helps Product Managers communicate product decisions through concise, evidence-backed written memos.

The repository improves decision quality by improving decision communication. It gives teams reusable frameworks and templates for making recommendations clear, trade-offs explicit, evidence inspectable, and decisions easier to review.

## Executive Summary

Good product decisions often fail when they are communicated poorly.

This repository provides a lightweight decision-writing system for Product Managers who need to align executives, engineering, design, data, go-to-market teams, and cross-functional stakeholders around a clear recommendation.

The goal is not to write longer documents. The goal is to make product judgment visible in fewer words.

## Problem Statement

Important product decisions are often trapped in meetings, Slack threads, slide decks, or undocumented assumptions.

When decisions are not written clearly, teams:

- Repeat debates.
- Lose context.
- Hide trade-offs.
- Confuse opinion with evidence.
- Miss alternatives.
- Avoid explicit decision requests.
- Struggle to learn from outcomes.

## Why This Repository Exists

Product teams need a practical way to communicate decisions before, during, and after execution.

This repository exists to help Product Managers write decision memos that are short enough to read, structured enough to review, and evidence-backed enough to improve decision quality.

## Target Audience

**Primary**

- Product Managers
- Product leaders
- Cross-functional product teams

**Secondary**

- Engineering managers
- Design leads
- Founders
- Strategy teams
- Interviewers evaluating product thinking

## Repository Philosophy

- Recommendation first.
- Evidence before opinion.
- Trade-offs made explicit.
- Alternatives treated seriously.
- Assumptions named clearly.
- Decision request visible.
- Background kept proportional to the decision.
