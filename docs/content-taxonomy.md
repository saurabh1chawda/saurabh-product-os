# Content Taxonomy

Status: RC1 taxonomy  
Last updated: July 2026

## Executive Summary

The content taxonomy defines how Product OS artifacts become publishing outputs while preserving a clear single source of truth.

Each output should belong to one primary category. This keeps publishing consistent, reduces duplication, and helps audiences understand what kind of value they are receiving.

## Taxonomy Overview

| Category | Primary Purpose | Best Source Assets | Primary Audience |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Insights | Teach a product leadership lesson | Executive Brief, Operating Principles, Product Leadership Briefs | Recruiters, hiring managers, product leaders |
| Frameworks | Explain a repeatable decision model | AI Product Playbook, AI Prioritization Framework, Decision Operating System | PMs, AI PMs, product leaders |
| Toolkits | Help someone perform product work | Product Discovery Toolkit, Decision Memo Template | PMs, product teams, workshop participants |
| Case Studies | Show evidence through a real decision | Product Leadership Briefs | Hiring managers, product leaders, interviewers |
| Announcements | Signal progress or release maturity | Product OS releases, GitHub repository launches | Recruiters, GitHub visitors, product community |

## Insights

Use Insights when the goal is to teach a concise product leadership lesson.

Best for:

- LinkedIn posts.
- Newsletter sections.
- Interview prep.
- Speaking introductions.

Use when:

- A decision produced a reusable principle.
- A recruiter needs quick context.
- A Product Leadership Brief has one lesson worth extracting.

Avoid when:

- The audience needs a template or step-by-step artifact.
- The source evidence is not yet documented.

## Frameworks

Use Frameworks when the output explains how to make a repeatable product decision.

Best for:

- GitHub repositories.
- Blog posts.
- Workshops.
- Product leader discussions.

Use when:

- The concept can guide decisions beyond one company.
- The method has clear inputs, trade-offs, and outputs.
- The framework links to Product OS or a flagship repository.

Avoid when:

- The idea is only a personal opinion.
- The artifact is actually a template or checklist.

## Toolkits

Use Toolkits when the audience needs a practical asset they can reuse.

Best for:

- GitHub.
- Workshops.
- Product coaching.
- Newsletter downloads.

Use when:

- The artifact helps someone perform discovery, prioritization, planning, or executive communication.
- The output has a clear fill-in structure.
- The asset can stand independently from Product OS.

Avoid when:

- The content only explains a concept.
- The artifact requires too much missing context to be useful.

## Case Studies

Use Case Studies when the goal is to show product judgment through a real decision.

Product OS uses Product Leadership Briefs rather than traditional case studies. Publishing should preserve that distinction.

Best for:

- Hiring manager evaluation.
- Interview preparation.
- LinkedIn decision threads.
- Speaking examples.

Use when:

- The decision has context, options, trade-offs, outcomes, and lessons.
- The evidence reinforces role fit.
- The output links back to the full Product Leadership Brief.

Avoid when:

- The story becomes a company-by-company resume recap.
- The output lacks a clear decision.

## Announcements

Use Announcements when a product, repository, framework, or release reaches a new maturity level.

Best for:

- LinkedIn launch posts.
- GitHub release notes.
- Newsletter updates.
- Portfolio update notes.

Use when:

- A new flagship repository becomes usable.
- A framework receives a worked example.
- A Product OS page becomes a stronger recruiter entry point.
- A release materially improves public value.

Avoid when:

- The update is too small to create audience value.
- The announcement does not include a useful next step.

## Classification Rules

- Pick one primary category per output.
- Add a secondary category only when it changes distribution.
- Link every output to the canonical source.
- Do not publish unsupported claims.
- Do not create a new category for every format.

## Recommended Category Mix

| Time Horizon | Mix |
| --- | --- |
| Weekly | Insight or small framework extraction |
| Monthly | One toolkit, case study rotation, or newsletter |
| Quarterly | Release announcement or portfolio-level synthesis |

## Operating Principle

Classify by visitor value, not by internal artifact type.
