# Distribution Rules

Status: RC1 distribution model  
Last updated: July 2026

## Executive Summary

Distribution rules define repeatable paths from flagship artifacts into acquisition channels.

Every distribution path should route qualified attention back to Product OS or the relevant GitHub repository. The goal is not to post everywhere. The goal is to match each artifact to the channels where it creates the most qualified trust.

## Core Rule

One flagship artifact should produce multiple distribution paths, but every path should point back to one canonical source.

## Executive Brief Distribution

Use when:

- Introducing Product OS to recruiters.
- Updating LinkedIn Featured assets.
- Routing GitHub visitors to the executive context.
- Sharing a pre-interview overview.

Distribution path:

```text
LinkedIn / GitHub / recruiter outreach
  |
Executive Brief
  |
Professional Profile or role-fit Product Leadership Brief
  |
Contact
```

Rules:

- Always use the Executive Brief as the fastest entry point.
- Do not send first-time recruiters directly to long-form frameworks.
- Use Contact as the conversion path.

## Product Leadership Brief Distribution

Use when:

- Demonstrating role-fit evidence.
- Publishing decision lessons.
- Preparing for hiring manager conversations.

Distribution path:

```text
Decision insight
  |
Role-fit Product Leadership Brief
  |
Operating Principles / AI Product Playbook
  |
Contact
```

Role-fit routing:

- Logix -> AI Platforms & Modernization.
- Mahindra Comviva -> Payments & Reliability.
- Simplilearn -> Growth & Product-Led Growth.
- JoVE -> Discovery & Enterprise SaaS.

Rules:

- Lead with the decision, not the employer.
- Include the role-fit signal.
- Link to the full brief, not a duplicated summary.

## AI Product Playbook Distribution

Use when:

- Publishing AI product judgment.
- Explaining lifecycle, prioritization, trust, or measurement.
- Supporting AI PM role fit.

Distribution path:

```text
AI product insight
  |
AI Product Playbook
  |
Related framework or Product Leadership Brief
  |
Professional Profile / Contact
```

Rules:

- Avoid AI hype.
- Tie AI to customer workflows, trust, and business outcomes.
- Use GitHub for reusable artifacts.

## AI Prioritization Framework Distribution

Use when:

- Helping PMs decide whether AI deserves investment.
- Supporting workshops or roadmap conversations.
- Publishing anti-patterns.

Distribution path:

```text
AI opportunity lesson
  |
AI Prioritization Framework
  |
AI Product Playbook
  |
Logix or related evidence
```

Rules:

- Emphasize decision quality over enthusiasm.
- Include when AI should not be prioritized.
- Link to worked examples when available.

## Product Discovery Toolkit Distribution

Use when:

- Publishing discovery lessons.
- Supporting PM community value.
- Demonstrating customer evidence discipline.

Distribution path:

```text
Discovery insight
  |
Product Discovery Toolkit
  |
JoVE Product Leadership Brief
  |
Executive Brief or Contact
```

Rules:

- Focus on evidence quality.
- Avoid generic research advice.
- Link toolkit use back to product decisions.

## Decision Memo Template Distribution

Use when:

- Publishing executive communication lessons.
- Supporting product council, investment, or roadmap discussions.
- Demonstrating structured product leadership.

Distribution path:

```text
Decision writing lesson
  |
Decision Memo Template
  |
Product Leadership Briefs
  |
Operating Principles
```

Rules:

- Lead with the decision request.
- Show why executive readability matters.
- Avoid turning memos into long documentation.

## Channel Sequencing

Preferred launch sequence for a mature artifact:

1. Update canonical source.
2. Publish LinkedIn insight.
3. Update GitHub README or release notes if applicable.
4. Add newsletter or blog depth.
5. Convert into workshop or speaking angle if the artifact creates repeated interest.

## Anti-Patterns

- Posting the same message across all channels.
- Sending recruiters to GitHub before Executive Brief.
- Publishing a framework without a clear next step.
- Creating channel-specific facts that do not exist in Product OS.
- Treating broad reach as success without evidence engagement.

## Operating Principle

Distribute by audience intent, not by channel availability.
