# Hidden Hiring Signals

## Purpose

Hidden hiring signals are implicit expectations inside a job description. They help the candidate prepare positioning and interview stories, but they are not stated facts.

Every hidden signal must include:

- Supporting JD phrases.
- Confidence score.
- Explanation.
- Resume implications.
- Interview implications.
- Evidence gap if applicable.

## Signal Catalog

| Hidden Signal | Supporting Phrases | Resume Implication | Interview Implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| High ambiguity | ambiguous, undefined, build from scratch, emerging | Highlight problem framing and discovery | Prepare ambiguity story |
| Zero-to-one ownership | 0->1, incubate, MVP, launch new | Highlight MVP and launch evidence | Explain first-principles prioritization |
| Operating at scale | scale, millions, global, enterprise-wide | Highlight throughput, reliability, adoption | Discuss systems trade-offs |
| Executive influence | executives, leadership team, board, VP | Highlight decision memos and executive communication | Prepare concise trade-off narrative |
| Deep IC expectations | hands-on, own end-to-end, individual contributor | Highlight execution and craft depth | Show direct product work |
| Player-coach scope | mentor, lead, coach, set standards | Highlight leadership without overclaiming | Discuss coaching style |
| Matrix leadership | cross-functional, partner teams, dependencies | Highlight alignment and influence | Explain conflict resolution |
| Technical depth | APIs, architecture, data, AI/ML, reliability | Highlight technical fluency | Prepare technical trade-off examples |
| Customer obsession | customers, users, research, feedback | Highlight discovery evidence | Explain customer learning |
| Data fluency | metrics, analytics, experiments, SQL | Highlight quantified outcomes | Discuss metric choice |
| Commercial ownership | revenue, pricing, profitability, business case | Highlight monetization evidence | Explain business impact |
| Platform thinking | platform, infrastructure, internal customers | Highlight platform decisions | Explain leverage and trade-offs |
| Operational maturity | cadence, process, governance, quality | Highlight operating systems | Discuss repeatability |
| Regulatory sensitivity | compliance, risk, policy, finance | Highlight risk-aware decisions | Avoid overstating expertise |

## Confidence Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 80-100 | Multiple strong phrases support the inference |
| 60-79 | One strong phrase or multiple weak phrases |
| 40-59 | Plausible but weak; human review required |
| 0-39 | Do not use |

## Rules

- Hidden signals must be labeled as inferences.
- Do not convert hidden signals into resume claims.
- If no evidence exists, report a gap.
- If evidence is a simulation or prototype, label it clearly.
