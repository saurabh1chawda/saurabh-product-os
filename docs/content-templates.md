# Content Templates

Status: RC1 template library  
Last updated: July 2026

## Executive Summary

These templates standardize how Product Leadership Platform assets become channel-ready content drafts.

Each template defines purpose, structure, CTA, and source requirements. The templates are production inputs, not final published content.

## Template Rules

- Use one canonical source artifact.
- Keep one key idea.
- Preserve all source facts.
- Include one CTA.
- Write for the channel.

## LinkedIn Insight

Purpose:

- Share one product leadership lesson in a fast, useful format.

Structure:

1. Hook: surprising or practical product observation.
2. Context: source artifact or decision.
3. Lesson: what other product leaders can use.
4. Evidence: one metric, trade-off, or outcome.
5. CTA: read source artifact or ask a discussion question.

CTA:

- Read the Executive Brief.
- Review the Product Leadership Brief.
- Explore the framework.

Source requirements:

- Source artifact link.
- One validated metric or decision.
- Clear audience.

## LinkedIn Carousel

Purpose:

- Turn a framework or decision into a visual sequence.

Structure:

1. Cover: one clear promise.
2. Problem: why the topic matters.
3. Framework or decision model.
4. Key steps or trade-offs.
5. Example.
6. Takeaway.
7. CTA.

CTA:

- Explore the full framework.
- Review the full brief.

Source requirements:

- Framework or Product Leadership Brief.
- Slide-level claims validated against source.
- One visual structure.

## LinkedIn Article

Purpose:

- Explain a product leadership lesson with enough depth for senior audiences.

Structure:

1. Opening thesis.
2. Business context.
3. Decision or framework.
4. Trade-offs.
5. Outcome or reusable lesson.
6. Related Product OS source.

CTA:

- Read the full Product OS source.

Source requirements:

- Source artifact.
- Evidence.
- Related resources.

## Newsletter

Purpose:

- Provide a curated product leadership lesson and next read.

Structure:

1. Short intro.
2. Main lesson.
3. Practical application.
4. Related source.
5. One reflection question.

CTA:

- Continue with the related Product OS or GitHub asset.

Source requirements:

- One source artifact.
- One practical takeaway.
- One next resource.

## Blog

Purpose:

- Provide deeper explanation of a framework, decision, or operating principle.

Structure:

1. Thesis.
2. Problem.
3. Source context.
4. Framework or decision.
5. Example.
6. Lessons.
7. Next step.

CTA:

- Explore the canonical source or reusable repository.

Source requirements:

- Canonical source.
- Supporting evidence.
- Related links.

## GitHub Release

Purpose:

- Explain what changed in a flagship repository and how visitors can use it.

Structure:

1. Release title.
2. What changed.
3. Why it matters.
4. What is usable now.
5. Related Product OS context.
6. Next release direction.

CTA:

- Use the repository.
- Read the Product OS context.

Source requirements:

- Repository.
- Status.
- Release scope.
- Related Product OS page.

## Framework Announcement

Purpose:

- Introduce a reusable framework without overexplaining it.

Structure:

1. Problem the framework solves.
2. Who it is for.
3. What it helps decide.
4. Core dimensions.
5. Example use case.
6. CTA.

CTA:

- Explore the framework.

Source requirements:

- Framework page or repository.
- Target audience.
- Use case.

## Case Study Summary

Purpose:

- Summarize a Product Leadership Brief for quick evaluation.

Structure:

1. Decision headline.
2. Business context.
3. Options considered.
4. Decision made.
5. Trade-off.
6. Outcome.
7. Reusable principle.

CTA:

- Review the Product Leadership Brief.

Source requirements:

- Product Leadership Brief.
- Validated metrics.
- Related role-fit label.

## Interview Story

Purpose:

- Prepare a concise story for interviews without turning it into a resume recap.

Structure:

1. Situation.
2. Decision.
3. Trade-off.
4. Action.
5. Outcome.
6. What I learned.
7. What I would do differently.

CTA:

- Reference the related Product Leadership Brief.

Source requirements:

- Product Leadership Brief or Professional Profile section.
- Metrics.
- Role relevance.

## Workshop Description

Structure:

1. Workshop title.
2. Audience.
3. Problem.
4. Learning outcomes.
5. Agenda.
6. Materials.
7. Expected outputs.

CTA:

- Use the toolkit or framework.

Source requirements:

- Exercise structure.
- Expected outputs.

## Conference Abstract

Purpose:

- Pitch a talk based on Product Leadership Platform evidence.

Structure:

1. Talk title.
2. Audience problem.
3. Core argument.
4. Evidence or example.
5. What attendees will learn.
6. Why now.

CTA:

Source requirements:

- Reusable principle.
- Audience fit.

## Podcast Outline

Purpose:

- Prepare a structured conversation around product leadership themes.

Structure:

1. Episode thesis.
2. Short bio.
3. Key discussion themes.
4. Product decision examples.
5. Practical takeaways.
6. Listener next step.

CTA:

- Read the Executive Brief or explore Product OS.

Source requirements:

- Executive Brief.
- Product Leadership Brief examples.
- AI Product Playbook or toolkit links when relevant.
