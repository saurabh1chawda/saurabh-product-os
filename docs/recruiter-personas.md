# Recruiter Personas

Status: RC1 personas  
Last updated: July 2026

## Executive Summary

Recruiter experience should be designed around time, context, and evaluation depth. Different visitors need different evidence, but all should reach the same conclusion quickly: whether Saurabh is worth interviewing for a senior product role.

## Persona 1: Agency Recruiter

### Goals

- Quickly understand role fit.
- Identify keywords and seniority signals.
- Find contact, LinkedIn, and profile links fast.
- Decide whether to submit or shortlist.

### Questions

- What roles does he fit?
- Is he senior enough?
- Does he have AI/platform/growth evidence?
- Where is the profile/contact information?

### Preferred Navigation Path

1. LinkedIn.
2. Executive Brief.
3. Professional Profile.
4. Contact / LinkedIn.

### Experience Need

Short, low-friction, high-confidence path. Avoid deep framework pages as first destination.

## Persona 2: Internal Recruiter

### Goals

- Validate fit against an active role.
- Collect evidence for hiring manager intake.
- Understand career progression.
- Confirm communication and contact details.

### Questions

- Can I explain this candidate to the hiring manager?
- Which evidence maps to the role?
- Does he have measurable outcomes?
- Is there a concise executive summary?

### Preferred Navigation Path

1. Executive Brief.
2. Professional Profile.
3. Relevant Product Leadership Brief.
4. GitHub profile if role values artifacts.
5. Contact.

### Experience Need

Role-fit labels and evidence cards that make forwarding easy.

## Persona 3: Hiring Manager

### Goals

- Evaluate product judgment before interview.
- Understand how Saurabh makes decisions under ambiguity.
- See evidence of outcomes and trade-offs.
- Prepare deeper interview questions.

### Questions

- Can he own a complex product?
- How does he prioritize?
- How does he work with engineering and stakeholders?
- What decisions prove his judgment?

### Preferred Navigation Path

1. Executive Brief.
2. Product Leadership Operating Principles.
3. Relevant Product Leadership Brief.
4. AI Product Playbook or Decision OS.
5. Professional Profile.

### Experience Need

Evidence depth, decision structure, and clear relationship between claims and outcomes.

## Persona 4: VP Product / CPO

### Goals

- Assess leadership maturity.
- Understand strategic judgment.
- Evaluate AI/platform/product-system thinking.
- Decide whether the candidate is worth senior leadership time.

### Questions

- Does he think beyond features?
- Can he sequence complex product bets?
- Can he communicate trade-offs clearly?
- Does his experience transfer to our context?

### Preferred Navigation Path

1. Executive Brief.
2. Product Leadership Operating Principles.
3. Logix or Simplilearn Product Leadership Brief.
4. AI Product Playbook.
5. GitHub flagship repository if artifact depth matters.

### Experience Need

Executive-level synthesis, evidence-backed principles, and minimal marketing language.

## Persona Design Implications

- Agency recruiters need fast profile and contact paths.
- Internal recruiters need role-fit evidence they can forward.
- Hiring managers need decision-quality evidence.
- VPs/CPOs need principles, trade-offs, and strategic transferability.

## Recommended Default Path

For unknown visitors, route to:

```text
Executive Brief
  |
Professional Profile
  |
Relevant Product Leadership Brief
  |
Contact
```
