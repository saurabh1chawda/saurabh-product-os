# Channel Strategy

Status: RC1 acquisition model  
Last updated: July 2026

## Executive Summary

The channel strategy defines how qualified audiences discover the Product Leadership Platform and move toward Product OS as the canonical destination.

Each channel has a different job. LinkedIn creates reach and professional context. GitHub proves artifact quality. Product OS converts attention into trust. Newsletter, blog, workshops, podcasts, and conference talks deepen credibility and reuse.

## LinkedIn

Primary audience:

- Recruiters.
- Hiring managers.
- Product leaders.
- AI Product Managers.

Purpose:

- Create qualified professional discovery and route visitors to the Executive Brief.

Strengths:

- Strong recruiter visibility.
- Fast feedback.
- Natural place for product leadership ideas.

Limitations:

- Short attention span.
- Engagement can reward broad takes over precise product judgment.
- Content is not a durable source of truth.

Best content types:

- Product leadership insights.
- Brief lessons.
- Framework previews.
- Launch notes.

Success metrics:

- LinkedIn profile visits.
- Product OS clicks.
- Executive Brief views.
- Qualified recruiter or hiring manager DMs.

## GitHub

Primary audience:

- Engineering leaders.
- Product managers.
- AI practitioners.
- Hiring managers evaluating technical collaboration.

Purpose:

- Demonstrate reusable artifacts and implementation-quality product thinking.

Strengths:

- Strong credibility for frameworks, templates, examples, and documentation.
- Supports developer relations and technical trust.

Limitations:

- Not every recruiter will inspect GitHub deeply.
- Repositories need excellent README navigation.

Best content types:

- Flagship repositories.
- Templates.
- Framework releases.
- Worked examples.

Success metrics:

- GitHub profile visits.
- Repository visits.
- Stars and forks.
- Product OS links clicked from README files.

## Product OS

Primary audience:

- Recruiters.
- Hiring managers.
- VP Product / CPO.
- Product leaders.

Purpose:

- Convert discovery into interview confidence.

Strengths:

- Canonical evidence.
- Strong recruiter paths.
- Clear business outcomes and product judgment.

Limitations:

- Needs acquisition channels to bring qualified visitors.

Best content types:

- Executive Brief.
- Professional Profile.
- Product Leadership Briefs.
- AI Product Playbook.
- Operating Principles.

Success metrics:

- Executive Brief views.
- Product Leadership Brief engagement.
- Profile views.
- Contact conversions.

## Newsletter

Primary audience:

- Product managers.
- Product leaders.
- AI PMs.

Purpose:

- Create a repeat audience that learns from Product OS artifacts over time.

Strengths:

- Owned relationship.
- Better for synthesis and recurring themes.

Limitations:

- Requires consistent delivery.
- Slower audience growth.

Best content types:

- Monthly theme summaries.
- Framework lessons.
- Case study rotations.

Success metrics:

- Opens.
- Click-through to Product OS and GitHub.
- Replies.
- Returning visitors.

## Blog

Primary audience:

- Product managers.
- AI PMs.
- Product leaders.

Purpose:

- Provide evergreen discovery and deeper explanations.

Strengths:

- Long-form clarity.
- Searchable.
- Good for framework education.

Limitations:

- Higher editorial effort.
- Needs strong source discipline.

Best content types:

- Framework explainers.
- Decision essays.
- Toolkit guides.

Success metrics:

- Organic visits.
- Source artifact clicks.
- Time on page.
- Returning visitors.

## Workshops

Primary audience:

- Product teams.
- Product managers.
- AI PMs.

Purpose:

- Turn Product Leadership Platform artifacts into practical learning experiences.

Strengths:

- High trust.
- Practical value.
- Strong artifact reuse.

Limitations:

- Time-intensive.
- Lower scale.

Best content types:

- Discovery sprint.
- AI prioritization workshop.
- Decision memo review.

Success metrics:

- Attendance.
- Follow-up requests.
- Template usage.
- Product OS visits after session.

## Podcasts

Primary audience:

- Product leaders.
- Founders.
- Recruiters.

Purpose:

- Humanize product leadership philosophy and create top-of-funnel discovery.

Strengths:

- Personal and conversational.
- Good for storytelling and principles.

Limitations:

- Less direct measurement.
- Lower control over framing.

Best content types:

- Operating principles.
- Product decision stories.
- AI product judgment.

Success metrics:

- Referral visits.
- Inbound messages.
- Mentioned episodes in conversations.

## Conference Talks

Primary audience:

- Product leaders.
- AI practitioners.
- Executives.

Purpose:

- Establish authority around product leadership and AI product judgment.

Strengths:

- High credibility.
- Strong network effects.
- Good for partnerships and senior conversations.

Limitations:

- Infrequent.
- Requires preparation and selection.

Best content types:

- AI Product Operating System.
- Product Leadership Brief lessons.
- Decision quality under uncertainty.

Success metrics:

- Invitations.
- Follow-up conversations.
- Product OS visits.
- Workshop requests.

## Operating Principle

Each channel should create a useful entry point, but Product OS remains the destination for trust and conversion.
