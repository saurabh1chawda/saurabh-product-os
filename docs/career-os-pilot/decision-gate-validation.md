# Career OS Pilot Decision Gate Validation

## Purpose

This document validates the six mandatory decision gates using the synthetic ORR rehearsal.

## Gate Review

| Gate | Decision Quality | Evidence Quality | Approval Clarity | Pass/Fail Criteria | Risk of Ambiguity | Validation Result |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Gate 1 - Proceed / Reject Opportunity | Strong | Sufficient | Clear | Clear enough for pilot | Low | PASS |
| Gate 2 - Proceed with Resume Generation | Strong | Strong | Clear | Clear | Low | PASS |
| Gate 3 - QA Approval | Strong | Strong | Clear | Clear but multi-factor | Medium | PASS WITH OBSERVATIONS |
| Gate 4 - Ready for Submission | Strong | Sufficient | Clear | Clear | Low | PASS |
| Gate 5 - Interview Complete | Strong | Sufficient | Clear | Clear | Low | PASS |
| Gate 6 - Pilot Learning Captured | Strong | Sufficient | Clear | Clear but subjective learning threshold | Medium | PASS WITH OBSERVATIONS |

## Observations

Gate 1 is lightweight and appropriate. It protects time without creating excessive process before JD capture.

Gate 2 is well positioned after JD Intelligence. It prevents resume generation when the fit, evidence, or gaps do not justify the effort.

Gate 3 is the most complex gate because it includes factual integrity, ATS readiness, recruiter scan, human writing, and link verification. This is correct for pilot quality, but it may be the highest-friction gate.

Gate 4 correctly separates internal readiness from external submission. It reinforces human approval for all external actions.

Gate 5 correctly prevents interviews from remaining in an ambiguous state after completion.

Gate 6 is valuable because it turns each application into product learning. The phrase "enough product learning" may require calibration after the first five applications.

## Validation Decision

PASS WITH OBSERVATIONS
