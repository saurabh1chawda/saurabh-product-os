# Publishing Metrics

Status: RC1 metrics model  
Last updated: July 2026

## Executive Summary

Publishing success should be measured by qualified opportunity creation, not content volume.

The Product Leadership Platform exists to make product judgment discoverable, credible, and useful. Publishing metrics should show whether the right people are finding the right evidence and taking meaningful next steps.

## North Star Metric

Qualified Interview Opportunities

Definition:

Recruiter, hiring manager, product leader, founder, or executive interactions that indicate credible hiring interest after engaging with Product OS, GitHub, or derived publishing content.

Examples:

- Interview request.
- Recruiter outreach referencing Product OS.
- Hiring manager referencing a Product Leadership Brief.
- LinkedIn message asking for role fit.
- Email inquiry after viewing Executive Brief or GitHub assets.

## Supporting Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Executive Brief views | Visits to `/executive` | Measures adoption of the fastest recruiter entry point |
| GitHub profile visits | Visits to the GitHub profile README | Measures interest in reusable artifacts |
| Repository stars | Stars across flagship repositories | Measures public artifact credibility |
| LinkedIn profile visits | Profile visits after publishing | Measures top-of-funnel interest |
| Contact conversions | Email, LinkedIn, or contact CTA clicks | Measures interview intent |
| Returning visitors | Visitors who return after first session | Measures sustained interest |
| Content reuse rate | Templates, frameworks, or repos reused, forked, downloaded, or referenced | Measures practical value |

## Channel Metrics

### Product OS

Track:

- Homepage to Executive Brief clicks.
- Executive Brief to Professional Profile clicks.
- Executive Brief to Product Leadership Brief clicks.
- Product Leadership Brief to Contact clicks.
- Profile to Contact clicks.

Interpretation:

Product OS is working when visitors move from summary to evidence to contact with minimal friction.

### GitHub

Track:

- GitHub profile visits.
- Repository views.
- Stars.
- Forks.
- Template downloads or copies.
- Product OS link clicks from README files.

Interpretation:

GitHub is working when reusable artifacts create credibility and return traffic to Product OS.

### LinkedIn

Track:

- Profile visits.
- Connection requests from relevant roles.
- Comments from product leaders or hiring audiences.
- Clicks to Product OS.
- Messages referencing specific artifacts.

Interpretation:

LinkedIn is working when posts create qualified curiosity, not just reactions.

### Blog / Newsletter

Track:

- Opens.
- Click-through to Product OS or GitHub.
- Returning subscribers.
- Replies.
- Saved or shared issues.

Interpretation:

Long-form publishing is working when it deepens trust and drives readers to source artifacts.

### Workshops / Speaking

Track:

- Attendee requests for templates.
- Product OS visits after session.
- Follow-up conversations.
- Reuse of frameworks.
- Invitations for additional sessions.

Interpretation:

Workshops and talks are working when the audience uses the artifacts after the session.

## Metric Hierarchy

```text
Qualified Interview Opportunities
  |
Contact conversions
  |
Executive Brief and evidence engagement
  |
GitHub and source artifact usage
  |
Publishing reach and discovery
```

Reach is useful only when it creates evidence engagement or qualified action.

## Reporting Cadence

| Cadence | Review |
| --- | --- |
| Weekly | Published outputs, channel signals, contact actions |
| Monthly | Executive Brief engagement, repository growth, content reuse |
| Quarterly | Qualified opportunities, platform positioning, publishing themes |

## Quality Signals

Track qualitative signals alongside metrics:

- Recruiters mention the Executive Brief.
- Hiring managers mention a Product Leadership Brief.
- Product leaders reference an operating principle.
- Engineering leaders reference GitHub artifacts.
- Interviewers ask deeper questions because Product OS reduced basic context-setting.

## Metric Anti-Patterns

- Optimizing for post impressions without qualified follow-through.
- Treating stars as a proxy for hiring interest.
- Publishing frequently without increasing source artifact engagement.
- Counting all contact clicks equally without role-fit context.
- Creating channel-specific facts that cannot be traced to Product OS.

## Success Definition

The publishing system is working when it increases the number and quality of hiring conversations while also making Product OS and GitHub more discoverable as reusable product leadership assets.
