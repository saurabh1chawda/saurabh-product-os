# Content Quality Score

Status: RC1 scoring model  
Last updated: July 2026

## Executive Summary

The Content Quality Score evaluates whether a content draft is ready for publishing handoff.

The model prioritizes accuracy, source traceability, audience value, and reusability over volume or polish alone.

## Scoring Model

Score each dimension from 1 to 5.

Maximum score: 50.

| Dimension | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Source Accuracy | Does the draft match the canonical source? |
| Single Idea Clarity | Is there one clear idea? |
| Audience Fit | Does it answer a real audience question? |
| Evidence Strength | Are claims supported by metrics, decisions, or artifacts? |
| Practical Value | Can the audience use the lesson? |
| CTA Clarity | Is the next step obvious? |
| Channel Fit | Does it feel native to the channel? |
| Reusability | Can the idea become future content or assets? |
| Tone Quality | Is the tone executive, practical, and credible? |
| Risk Control | Are unsupported claims, hype, and confidentiality risks removed? |

## Score Interpretation

| Score | Recommendation |
| ---: | --- |
| 45-50 | Ready for publishing handoff |
| 38-44 | Strong draft; revise small gaps |
| 30-37 | Needs editorial and evidence improvement |
| 20-29 | Rebrief before drafting further |
| 10-19 | Reject or return to source artifact |

## Dimension Guidance

### 1. Source Accuracy

Score high when:

- All facts match Product OS or the relevant repository.
- Metrics are unchanged.
- Context is preserved.

Score low when:

- Claims cannot be traced.
- The draft changes meaning.
- It introduces new facts.

### 2. Single Idea Clarity

Score high when:

- The idea can be summarized in one sentence.
- The hook, body, and CTA reinforce the same point.

Score low when:

- The draft explains multiple unrelated ideas.
- The reader cannot tell what to remember.

### 3. Audience Fit

Score high when:

- The audience is clear.
- The content answers a real need.
- The CTA matches the audience.

Score low when:

- The content is generic.
- It reads like internal documentation.

### 4. Evidence Strength

Score high when:

- The draft includes a validated decision, metric, trade-off, or artifact.

Score low when:

- The draft relies on opinion without evidence.

### 5. Practical Value

Score high when:

- The audience learns something usable.
- The output helps with a decision, review, interview, or workflow.

Score low when:

- The draft only promotes Product OS.

### 6. CTA Clarity

Score high when:

- There is one specific next action.

Score low when:

- Multiple CTAs compete.
- The CTA is vague.

### 7. Channel Fit

Score high when:

- LinkedIn is concise.
- GitHub is usable.
- Blog content has depth.
- Workshop content has outputs.

Score low when:

- The format feels copied from another channel.

### 8. Reusability

Score high when:

- The idea can become a future template, prompt, workshop, or speaking point.

Score low when:

- The output is too narrow or disposable.

### 9. Tone Quality

Score high when:

- The tone is calm, executive, practical, and evidence-driven.

Score low when:

- It sounds inflated, vague, or overly promotional.

### 10. Risk Control

Score high when:

- Confidentiality, unsupported claims, and AI hype are removed.

Score low when:

- The content includes sensitive details or unverified claims.

## Release Gate

Publishing handoff requires:

- Minimum total score: 38.
- Source Accuracy score: 5.
- Risk Control score: 5.
- CTA Clarity score: at least 4.

## Operating Principle

Quality is not polish. Quality is useful, accurate, source-backed content that earns trust.
